Philly Blockchain Tech

Talk Topic Ideas

At our first event, Ryan Findley led an awesome group discussion on what members wanted to learn about at future events. The result was this awesome list of possible talk topics. Group members are encouraged to give presentations at Philly Blockchain Tech meetings. If you have expertise in one of these areas, or simply want to research more and present your learnings to the group, consider picking a topic and giving a talk!

  • Developing and deploying Ethereum smart contracts in Solidity
  • Enterprise app interoperability with blockchains
  • Auditing Solidity: writing secure smart contracts
  • Modeling and testing smart contracts
  • Non-currency applications of blockchain
  • How to separate projects with technical merit from scams and money grabs
  • Environmental issues of mining, plus alternative methods: time proofs, proof of stake, proof of context, etc...
  • How can businesses of different sizes benefit from blockchain tech. How practical is it to use?
  • How does mining really work? What are the incentives? How does it secure the blockchain?
  • Whats going on with blockchain in Philly? Map what's happening in the Philadelphia area and all of PA?
  • Game Theory: what is it and how does it influence the design of blockchain based protocols?
  • What is "Stealth Mining"? Can it be used for social impact? For content monetization? Etc...
  • An economist's take on cryptocurrencies
  • A finance expert's take on cryptocurrencies
  • What would a blockchain bootcamp curriculum look like?
  • What is the bear case for blockchain? How could this technology fail? In what disciplines is blockchain a bad fit?
  • Blockchain project governance: how is this being done? What have the challenges been? What models might solve these?
  • How are cryptocurrencies effecting geopolitics? How might this change?
  • What are utility tokens and what purpose do they serve?
  • How does blockchain impact First Amendment rights and free-speech globally?
  • What is an oracle? Are there any working implementations? More generally, how do we decide who puts "off-chain" data on-chain?
  • Regulation: what has happened so far? How is it evolving? How might it effect the adoption of blockchain technology?
  • To what extend do blockchains enable truly nefarious use-cases? What can and should be done about that?
  • Micro-transactions on the blockchain
  • Machine to machine transactions and mesh networks
